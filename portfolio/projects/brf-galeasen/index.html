RoleUX Designer
Year2025
ClientBRF Galeasen
ToolsFigma · Plausible
Intro

A resident portal that actually gets used.

BRF Galeasen is a housing cooperative in Gothenburg with around 120 households. The board wanted a resident portal for announcements, shared laundry booking, and contact with the property manager. The previous system — a mailing list and a paper notice board — was being ignored by most residents under 40.

My work focused on the design of the portal and, critically, the instrumentation needed to understand whether it was actually being used.

Moodboard

Warm, civic, unfussy

The direction pulled from public service design — Swedish municipality sites, public transit apps, and the restrained typographic sensibility of civic publishing.

Story

Design the tracking with the product

Most internal tools are built without any way to measure whether they work. For this project, I wanted to design the analytics alongside the interface — defining up front what a healthy usage pattern would look like, and instrumenting for it from day one.

I set up a small event model in Plausible tracking four things: logins, announcement reads, laundry bookings, and manager contacts. The goal wasn't surveillance — it was to give the board a clear answer to "is this actually replacing the mailing list?"

The design itself stayed deliberately plain. A dashboard with four cards, a laundry booking calendar, and a simple announcements feed. The entire portal sits on four screens.

Result

Adoption in numbers

Within six weeks of launch, the portal had replaced the mailing list for most board-to-resident communication. The tracking dashboard is now a permanent part of the board's monthly review.
